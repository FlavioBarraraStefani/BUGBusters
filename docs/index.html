About Us

The BUGBusters study group is composed of three students enrolled in the Master’s Degree program in Artificial Intelligence at the University of Genoa, within the Data Visualization course taught by Professor Annalisa Barla.


The goal of the project is to provide an intuitive and informative representation of a dataset related to global terrorism, highlighting significant trends, patterns, and relationships. The work seeks to transform complex data into clear and meaningful visual information, supporting a deeper understanding of the analyzed phenomena.

Project Description and Data at hand

What are we talking about?

The Global Terrorism Database (GTD) is an event-level dataset, maintained by the University of Maryland, containing more than 200,000 records of terrorist attacks worldwide spanning between 1970 and 2020.
Data are compiled from a wide range of open sources: nonetheless, the GTD team integrates and standardizes these data to ensure consistency across the entire time span, conducting extensive quality control, retrospective coding of key variables, and cross-referencing with other terrorism databases to identify and include missing events.


What are we dealing with?

Each record in the GTD represents a single terrorist incident and includes detailed information such as the date and location of the attack, type of attack and weapon used, targets and victims, perpetrators and claims of responsibility, as well as casualties, consequences, and data sources.
A detailed description of the dataset can be found in the GTD website.
