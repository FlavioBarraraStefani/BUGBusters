Project Description and Data at hand

What are we talking about?

The Global Terrorism Database (GTD) is an event-level dataset containing more than 200,000 records of terrorist attacks that occurred worldwide between 1970 and 2020. It is maintained by the National Consortium for the Study of Terrorism and Responses to Terrorism (START) at the University of Maryland.
Data are compiled from a wide range of open sources, including news media, official reports, and academic publications. The GTD team integrates and standardizes these data to ensure consistency across the entire time span, conducting extensive quality control, retrospective coding of key variables, and cross-referencing with other terrorism databases to identify and include missing events.
Each record in the GTD represents a single terrorist incident and includes detailed information on the date and location of the attack, type of attack and weapon used, targets and victims, perpetrators and claims of responsibility, as well as casualties, consequences, and data sources. Events are included only if they meet specific definitional criteria of terrorism, emphasizing political, religious, or ideological motivations.

About Us

The BUGBusters study group is composed of three students enrolled in the Master’s Degree program in Artificial Intelligence at the University of Genoa, within the Data Visualization course taught by Professor Annalisa Barla.


The project aims to develop a visual analytics system for exploring and interpreting data related to global terrorism. By applying various data visualization techniques, the goal is to provide an intuitive and informative representation of the dataset, highlighting significant trends, patterns, and relationships. The work seeks to transform complex data into clear and meaningful visual information, supporting a deeper understanding of the analyzed phenomena.
